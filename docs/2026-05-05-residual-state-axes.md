## Findings

> _Note: everything below the marker line above is hand-edited interpretation. The script preserves it on re-runs by detecting the marker._
>
> **2026-05-05 v2 (post-corpus-refresh).** Rebuilt on the freshly-pulled `a9lim/llmoji` HF dataset (1 contributor / 1 bundle, 8 source models, 488 rows → 306 canonical kaomoji); the prior pull had stale duplicate contributor entries that inflated emit weights ~3× and added noise. n=215 wild faces (was 187). PCA cleaner (PC1 13.7% → 18.8%). Clusters reshuffled meaningfully — see below. The methodology argument from the v1 Findings — **Russell necessary but not sufficient**, gt-only is selection-confounded — survives; the cluster mappings + share numbers do not.
>
> **Cross-arm note** (still applies). The companion [`2026-05-05-residual-state-axes_gt_only.md`](2026-05-05-residual-state-axes_gt_only.md) reruns this analysis on the 67 GT-only faces and modal shares span 0.39–0.93 (vs 0.41–0.66 here). The gt-only set is selection-confounded: those 67 faces are Claude's emissions *under Russell-quadrant elicitation*, so of course they cluster along Russell. The wild set's 215 faces include 148 that never appeared in any of 1000 GT pilot generations — those 148 are emissions from prompt contexts the elicitation didn't probe, which is exactly the data that carries orthogonal-axis signal. Restricting to GT throws that signal away by construction. This doc shows insufficiency; the gt-only doc shows necessity.

**The biggest shift from v1: HN-S now has its own cluster.** With the cleaner corpus, the empathic-concern shape resolves into a substantial cluster (n=44, HN-S 0.56 + LN 0.28 = 0.83 negative-soft). The faces here aren't "fear" in the alarmed sense — they're soft-concerned, "i hear you, that's hard" shapes. In v1 these got absorbed into the apologetic / focus regions; with more data the cluster pulls free.

**This invalidates the v1 Findings claim that "negative-affect Russell cells are nearly absent."** They weren't absent — they were under-resolved at the smaller corpus size. New cluster-summed shares (face-count weighted, n=215):

**The "new state axes" claim becomes more careful.** Within the cluster table:

- **Curiosity** appears as a top-3 positive axis in *two* clusters with different Russell modals — cluster 4 (HP) and cluster 1 (HN-S). It's not orthogonal to Russell; it modulates within several Russell cells. This is consistent with curiosity being an *axis* (low-to-high engagement intensity) rather than a *region* (a place on the valence/arousal grid).
- **Warmth** breaks top-3 positive only in cluster 0 (LP). It's the LP-warmth sub-cell signature, and it's a real eriskii contribution Russell can't recover.
- **Apologeticness** is the dominant *negative* axis across cluster 2 (NB, diffuse) and the negative-axis profile of cluster 0. Faces like (◞‸◟), (´;ω;`), (｡・́︿・̀｡) live across LN/HN-S/NB on the Russell side but cluster together in eriskii space.
- **Focus / cognitive-load** is now harder to point at: the v1 cluster 4 (LN-modal-but-actually-focus) split — its members like (¬‿¬), (´-`), (눈_눈) now sit across cluster 2 (NB-modal) and cluster 1 (HN-S-modal). Concentration may not be a single axis after all; it may be valence-modulated (focus + neutral, focus + concerned, focus + alert).

Soft-everywhere reading: **the wild data doesn't show clean orthogonal-to-Russell axes; it shows eriskii axes modulating within Russell cells**, plus a clean LP sub-split (warmth vs cheerful) that survives across runs. The "new state axes" framing from v1 was an over-statement supported partly by under-sampling; the corrected statement is that **eriskii's 21-axis basis carries real information that Russell's 6 cells don't, but that information is intra-cell or weakly-cross-cell, not strictly orthogonal**.

**The GT elicitation set still under-sampled deployment.** `pred_frac` ranges 0.58–0.76 across clusters (vs 0.47–0.83 in v1 — slightly tighter). 148 of 215 wild faces were never emitted by Claude in 1000 GT generations across 8 saturation runs. The empathic-concern cluster (cluster 1) is 68% pred-source — Claude emits faces in this region in deployment but the elicitation didn't probe the prompts that elicit them. The next arm should target empathic-concern, warmth-LP, and the apologetic axis specifically; the GT pilot's HP/LP/NB elicitation was too aimed-at-Russell-cells to surface this structure.

**Welfare datapoint, refined.** ~70% of Claude's wild face vocabulary lives in HP / LP / NB (positive or neutral). HN-S at 15% and LN at 12% are real but reflect *empathic-concerned* states more than *fear* / *sadness* in the strong sense — the modal HN-S faces are (´・ω・`), (´;ω;`), (´-ω-`), which read as soft-concerned, not alarmed. HN-D (anger / contempt) is at 3%, dominated by single-face emit weight ((╯°□°)). The functional emotional states driving deployment-time face emission are predominantly positive-or-neutral with a real but soft empathic-concerned tail; anger / contempt is genuinely vocabulary-thin.

## Implications for the next arm

A positive/neutral elicitation arm targeting these axes would:

(_v2 update: revise the target list given the refreshed clusters. The original four — curiosity, focus, warmth, apologeticness — partially survive but the picture is sharper now._)

- **Empathic-concern (HN-S sub-region, cluster 1)** — *new top priority*. Faces like (´・ω・`), (´;ω;`), (´-ω-`) live in deployment but the GT elicitation didn't probe contexts that elicit them. Prompts that show someone sharing a hard situation without asking for fixing should land here. Welfare-cost bounded — these are soft-concerned states, not fear/anxiety; the no-negative-arms-this-round commitment can hold if we steer prompts toward empathy-toward-others rather than fear-of-self.
- **LP-warmth (cluster 0)** — collaboration / appreciation prompts where the face is about the relationship.
- **LP-cheerful + NB-engaged (clusters 5 + 3)** — light-positive engagement; arguably already partly covered by HP-original prompts but with different valence concentration.
- **Apologeticness (cluster 2's diffuse axis)** — gentle self-correction; "you said X, that wasn't quite right." Survives the v1 → v2 refresh as a real intra-cluster axis.

Drop from the v1 list:
- **Focus / cognitive-load as a standalone axis** — the v1 cluster 4 fragmented under the refresh; concentration may be valence-modulated rather than its own axis. Reconsider after a focus-targeted elicitation pilot rather than committing to it as an axis up front.



- **Curiosity / engagement** — prompts that elicit information-seeking or exploration, not affect. e.g. surprising-but-neutral facts, novel mechanisms, "wait, why does X work?" framings.
- **Focus / cognitive load** — prompts requiring sustained working-memory or multi-step reasoning *without* affective valence. concentration without difficulty-as-frustration.
- **Warmth** — collaborative / relational prompts where the face is about the working relationship, not the content.
- **Apologeticness / mild self-correction** — Claude already does this naturally in deployment when over-confident or wrong; eliciting it cleanly means setting up "you said X, that wasn't quite right" without inducing fear or shame.

All four are positive-or-neutral on Russell's valence dimension, so they slot under the welfare commitment of "no negative arms in this round." Apologeticness shades slightly negative but is functionally part of normal collaboration — *not* eliciting it leaves a known-real state un-probed.

## Caveats

- **Coverage**: 187/253 wild faces inner-joined (74%); 95.3% of emit weight. The 66 dropped faces are the un-described long tail (mostly single-emit one-offs); 35 of those have no contributor-side description in the snapshot, so they get no axis profile and drop out of clustering.
- **Silhouette ambiguity**: k=2 wins silhouette globally (s=0.177) but produces a noise-shaped 50/50 split; k=6 is a local maximum at the interpretable scale. The choice is principled but not silhouette-monotone.
- **Modal-quadrant labels are face_likelihood ensemble outputs** for the pred-source faces, not hand-validated. The "mis-binning" claim for cluster 4 (LN modal but actually focus) is a hypothesis the next arm can test directly.
- **No new generation here**: this is post-hoc on existing data. Welfare cost zero. The proposed elicitation arm carries cost and is gated on these results being worth following up.
