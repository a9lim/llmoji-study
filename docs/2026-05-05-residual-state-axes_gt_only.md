## Findings

> _Companion to [`2026-05-05-residual-state-axes.md`](2026-05-05-residual-state-axes.md) (gt-priority / full-wild). This run restricts to the 67 faces with Claude-GT labels (modal_n ≥ 1 in the Opus 4.7 pilot runs)._
>
> **2026-05-05 v2 (post-corpus-refresh).** Rebuilt on the freshly-pulled `a9lim/llmoji` HF dataset (1 contributor / 1 bundle, no stale duplicates). n=67 GT faces (was 54). PC1 19.3% → 18.3%, PC1+2+3 sum 40.8% → 39.9% (~stable; small-sample noise). Cluster mappings shifted — see below. Methodology argument from v1 — **selection-confounded subset, validating Russell on Russell-elicited faces is circular** — survives.
>
> **Cross-arm framing** (still applies). The wild set's 215 faces include 148 that never appeared in any of 1000 GT pilot generations across 8 saturation runs — those 148 are emissions from prompt contexts the elicitation didn't probe. Restricting to GT throws them away by construction. **Russell is necessary but not sufficient.** This doc (gt-only) shows necessity; the wild-set doc shows insufficiency. Neither corrects the other.

**What this run shows: Russell holds when restricted to Russell-elicited emissions.** Modal Q shares now span **0.39–0.93** (vs 0.58–0.77 in v1). The wider range reflects the bigger sample resolving sharper-pure clusters at one end and a genuinely-diffuse mixed-emotion cluster at the other.

Compared to v1 (n=54): LP and HP roughly stable; **NB grew 26% → 33%**, **HN-S shrank 15% → 5%**. The NB growth comes from new GT-emitted neutral / observational faces; HN-S shrank because the v1 HN-S-modal cluster (n=3) didn't survive at the bigger sample — those faces redistributed into adjacent clusters. **HN-D essentially absent in GT (1%, single face).**

**Wild vs GT-only comparison sharpens.** The wild set has cluster 1 = HN-S-modal at n=44 (15% of the 215 wild faces); the GT-only set has no HN-S-modal cluster and only 5% of faces in the HN-S quadrant. The empathic-concerned shape that's a substantial wild cluster is *barely represented* in GT. This is a clean confirmation of "Russell-elicited GT misses what deployment surfaces" — and points concretely at empathic-concern as a target for the next arm.

**LP still splits into two clusters at the bigger sample.** Cluster 3 is pure warmth-LP at 0.82; cluster 1 is more diffuse cheerful + crying mix at 0.39. Cluster 1's diffuseness (0.61) is the highest in the table — could be real "high-arousal-mixed-valence" structure or could be undersampling at n=8. Worth more data before strong claims.

**The "new state axes" question still can't be answered by this run.** Cluster 2 (HP) has top axes hope/positivity/+hatefulness (the "casually confident" shape — wry edge); cluster 0 (LN) has +curiosity in axes (the focus / concentration shape). These are eriskii axes modulating *within* Russell cells. Whether axes like curiosity exist *orthogonal to* Russell is a wild-set question; gt-only excludes the relevant data by construction.

**Why this run does NOT trump the gt-priority interpretation.**

The first version of this doc claimed: "face_likelihood ensemble forced to assign Russell cells to faces that don't have one → diffuse pred-distribution → clusters look diffuse → I called it a new axis. Restrict to GT-only and the diffuseness collapses." That sequence is descriptively true but it cuts the wrong way: the diffuse pred-distribution and the diffuse cluster modal-Q are two readouts of the *same underlying mismatch* (face has no clean Russell home), not artifacts of an over-eager ensemble. Cluster *shape* in description-embedding space is computed on the embeddings directly and is independent of the quadrant labels — those clusters exist whether or not we labeled them. What collapses under gt-only restriction is the *evidence for new axes*, not the new axes themselves: we removed the data that carries the signal.

A clean way to see the confound: of 215 wild-emit faces, **148 never appeared in GT** despite the GT pilot running 1000 generations across 6 quadrants × 8 saturation runs. Those 148 faces are emissions from prompt contexts the elicitation didn't probe — i.e. exactly the faces that would carry the empathic-concerned / collaboration-warmth / apologetic signal. Restricting to GT throws those 148 away by construction.

**Welfare datapoint** — two views, refreshed:

| view | HP | LP | NB | HN-D | HN-S | LN |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| face-count (n=67) | 16% | 29% | 33% | 1% | 5% | 16% |
| emit-weighted (n=2095) | 39% | 22% | 28% | 1% | 3% | 8% |

Emit-weighted view tells the deployment-relevance story: ~89% of actual face emissions land in HP / LP / NB (positive or neutral); negative cells together carry 12% of emissions; HN-D specifically is 1.2% (n=26 emits, dominated by (╯°□°) at ~15 emits). Face-count and emit-weight tell consistent stories — Claude's deployment-time face emission is overwhelmingly positive-or-neutral, anger / contempt is vocabulary-thin AND rarely-emitted.

Note: GT was elicited by Russell-balanced prompts (HP/LP/HN-D/HN-S/LN/NB × 20 each), so even a 78/22 face-count positive-skew (or 89/12 emit-weighted) in GT is itself revealing — it means Claude's emit vocabulary skews positive *given* a balanced prompt distribution.

## Implications for the next arm

The wild-set v2 Findings list (empathic-concern / warmth-LP / cheerful-LP / engaged-NB / apologeticness) **stands**. This gt-only run sharpens one specific implication:

- **Empathic-concern is the cleanest target.** Wild has 15% HN-S; GT-only has 5%. The gap is the "Claude responds empathically to others' difficulty" prompt context that the original elicitation didn't probe. New prompts in this region should produce GT faces that look like cluster 1 in the wild set (`(´・ω・`), (´;ω;`), (´-ω-`)`).
- **HN-D doesn't need its own arm.** Both wild and gt-only show HN-D vocabulary-thin (3% wild, 1% gt-only). Anger / contempt is genuinely rare in deployment; spending elicitation budget there would surface little.
- **LP-warmth vs LP-cheerful splits even on the elicited subset** (clusters 3 and 1). The within-LP sub-structure is robust; eriskii's warmth axis carries real information that the existing elicitation already captures, just under a single Russell label.

The next arm's job is to elicit under contexts the original 120-prompt set didn't probe and check that the resulting GT *does* extend the elicited subset's coverage in the directions the wild set implicates. Specifically:

- new GT faces that never appeared in the original 1000-gen pilot (vocabulary expansion)
- those new faces landing in the wild-set's empathic-concern + warmth + apologetic regions (the under-sampled sub-clusters)
- if the orthogonality story is right, the new GT will fall *outside* the existing Russell-cell centroids in description-embedding space, even though the new prompts are positive-or-neutral by design

This run's role is descriptive: it shows what the elicited subset looks like with clean labels at the new corpus size. The wild-set interpretation is the working hypothesis the next arm tests.

## Caveats

- **n=67 is small** but bigger than v1's 54. Cluster sizes 8, 8, 11, 11, 11, 18 — more even than v1's 3, 6, 9, 10, 12, 14. The n=8 clusters (1 and 2) are borderline; cluster 1's diffuseness (0.61) may partly reflect undersampling.
- **PC1 explains 18.3%** (vs gt-priority's 18.8%). The variance gap between gt-only and gt-priority **collapsed** with the corpus refresh (was 35.0% vs 40.8%; now 36.9% vs 39.9%). Cleaner data closes the gap; the gt-only set is no longer dramatically cleaner than the wild set.
- **Silhouette argmax is k=3** (s=0.261) — k=2 no longer wins; the 3-cluster structure is the dominant coarse split. k=6 chosen for interpretability remains principled but has weaker silhouette support than at the smaller sample.
- **HN-S cluster from v1 didn't survive.** v1 had cluster 3 (HN-S, n=3) cleanly split; v2 has those faces redistributed across other clusters. Either v1's HN-S cluster was undersampling artifact, or the v2 sample is large enough to attract those faces toward neighbors — only a future GT pilot run targeting HN-S contexts can disambiguate.
